Detailed Description

Timecode, timestamps, durations, and frame rates.

SMPTE Timecode (backed by libvtc), steady-clock TimeStamp, calendar-aware DateTime, Duration, ElapsedTimer for benchmarking, and FrameRate descriptors.

◆ ClockPauseMode

enum class promeki::ClockPauseMode
strong

Describes a clock's pause capability.

The base Clock applies the same delta-based pause accounting for both paused modes: at pause it snapshots the filtered raw value; on resume it adds raw_now - raw_at_pause to the paused-offset accumulator. The enum exists so callers can distinguish CannotPause (where Clock::setPause will error) and so consumers can reason about whether the underlying clock source actually stops.

Enumerator
CannotPause 

Clock::setPause returns NotSupported.

PausesRawKeepsRunning 

Pause is bookkeeping only; raw() keeps advancing.

PausesRawStops 

onPause(true) freezes raw(); onPause(false) resumes.

◆ PacingVerdict

enum class promeki::PacingVerdict
strong

Outcome of a single PacingGate::wait call.

The verdict is the gate's recommendation to the caller — it does not prescribe an action. Backends decide what to do based on what they can do (a sender with no cross-fade just drops on Skip; a sender with an SRC may compress the next block instead).

Enumerator
OnTime 

Slept until the deadline; proceed normally.

Late 

Past the deadline by less than the skip threshold; proceed without sleeping. No catch-up action is recommended — the drift is small enough to absorb.

Skip 

Past the deadline by at least the skip threshold but less than the reanchor threshold; the backend should drop / fast-forward this work item to bound the lag.

Reanchor 

Past the deadline by at least the reanchor threshold; the gap is unrecoverable, the gate has re-anchored to clock->now() so the next wait() restarts the timeline.
