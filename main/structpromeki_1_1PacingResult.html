◆ error

Error promeki::PacingResult::error = Error::Ok

Underlying clock error if the gate could not query now() or perform the sleep.

Errors are reported through this field rather than changing verdict so the caller sees both "what was the timing intent" and "what went wrong" in a single result. The verdict in an error case is PacingVerdict::OnTime by convention (no action recommended; the timeline has not advanced from the gate's view).

◆ skippedTicks

int promeki::PacingResult::skippedTicks = 0

Number of complete period intervals the gate is currently behind by, when verdict is PacingVerdict::Skip.

Zero for every other verdict. A backend with a cross-fade or SRC can use this count to collapse N blocks worth of drift into a single shorter block instead of dropping; backends without that capability ignore it and just drop the current work item once.

◆ slack

Duration promeki::PacingResult::slack

Difference between the deadline and the observed clock->now() at the wait point.

Positive means the deadline was in the future and the gate slept (the magnitude is roughly the sleep duration). Negative means the deadline had already passed by that much (the magnitude is the lag). Zero on the first wait after a re-anchor and on no-op (null clock) returns.
